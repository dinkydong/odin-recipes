Best Damn Chili

  1. 4 tablespoons olive oil
  2. 1 yellow onion, chopped
  3. 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  4. 1 Anaheim chile pepper, chopped
  5. 2 red jalapeno pepper, chopped
  6. 4 garlic cloves, minced
  7. 2 ½ pounds lean ground beef
  8. ¼ cup Worcestershire sauce
  9. 1 pinch garlic powder, or to taste
  10. 2 beef bouillon cubes
  11. 1 (12 fluid ounce) can or bottle light beer (such as Coors®)
  12. 1 (28 ounce) can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  13. 1 (14.5 ounce) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  14. 1 (12 ounce) can tomato paste
  15. ½ cup white wine
  16. 2 tablespoons chili powder
  17. 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  18. 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  19. 1 tablespoon chipotle pepper sauce
  20. 2 ½ teaspoons dried basil
  21. 1 ½ teaspoons smoked paprika
  22. 1 teaspoon salt
  23. ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  24. ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  25. 2 (16 ounce) cans dark red kidney beans (such as Bush's®)
  26. 1 cup sour cream
  27. 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  28. ½ teaspoon ground cumin
Step 1

Gather all ingredients.

Step 2

Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat; cook and stir onion, bell pepper, Anaheim pepper, jalapeno peppers, and garlic in the hot oil until softened.

Step 3

Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes.

Step 4

Add Worcestershire sauce and garlic powder. Crumble bouillon cubes over beef and add beer. Continue to cook , scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet, until liquid is hot, about 3 minutes.

Step 5

Stir beef mixture into pepper mixture.

Step 6

Stir crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and wine to the beef mixture. Season with chili powder, 2 tablespoons cumin, brown sugar, pepper sauce, basil, paprika, salt, oregano, and black pepper.

Step 7

Bring to a boil and reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer until meat and vegetables are very tender and flavors have developed in the chili, about 90 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 8

Mix kidney beans into beef and vegetables. Continue to simmer until beans are hot, about 30 minutes more.

Step 9

Blend sour cream, cilantro, and remaining 1/2 teaspoon cumin in a food processor until smooth. Serve sour cream mixture with chili.

Step 10

Serve hot and enjoy!
